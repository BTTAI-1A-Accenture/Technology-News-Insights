The race is held by the nonprofit Run for Myles, which was established in honor of Myles Helm who passed away last July. Helm was an accomplished runner and completed countless races including two Ironman World Championships and over 60 marathons. "Among all his achievements, the 20 marathons he ran in Palos Verdes held a special place in his heart. Fulfilling his dream of making Palos Verdes his home, Myles found immense joy in this city. Preserving its breathtaking beauty became a cause close to his heart," the nonprofit said. In Helm's honor, and coinciding with the City of Rancho Palos Verdes' 50th anniversary, runners are invited to participate in the race on Sept. 9. The starting point will be the Point Vicente Lighthouse and will take participants along a picturesque road with breathtaking views of the ocean. The event will kick off at 7 a.m. with registration open to the public and the 10K race will take off at 8 a.m. The 5K race will start at 8:15 and the 1K Kids' Fun Race will take place at 9:45 a.m. In addition to the races, attendees will enjoy music, food trucks and vendors to celebrate Helm and the city's 50th anniversary at 12:30 p.m. Those interested in participating can sign up at the race's webpage. The price to compete in the 10K is $55, the 5K is $50 and the Kids' Fun Run is $25. "By participating in Run For Myles, you play an integral role in preserving the parks and trails that made Palos Verdes so special to Myles. Together, we can carry forward his passion for this community and ensure its natural splendor endures for generations to come," the Helm family said.
