Expect road closures, detours, and traffic delays UGI Utilities, Inc. will begin a natural gas main replacement project in the city of Lancaster starting this week. Video above: The Susquehanna Valley's busiest roads and intersections.  The natural gas main replacement project will impact the following streets: Approximately 3,300 feet of non-contemporary main will be replaced, according to UGI. They say this project will also renew gas service lines to approximately 67 customers. Construction hours will take place from 9:00 a.m. to 2:30 p.m., Monday through Friday.  During construction hours, residents will experience road closures, detours, and traffic delays. Parking also will be restricted on those blocks during construction hours. Accommodations will be made for emergency vehicles. Part of this project will occur on the street that runs parallel to Clipper Magazine Stadium, home of the Lancaster Barnstormers. This project may cause traffic congestion during home game days. The project is expected to be completed by early September.
