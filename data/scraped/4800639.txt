The latest breaking updates, delivered straight to your email inbox.  A mountain lion was sighted again in southwest Omaha, according to Nebraska Game and Parks. Earlier this week, a Ring doorbell camera captured a mountain lion walking near 114th and Q streets in a neighborhood. According to Game and Parks, a mountain lion was caught on video in the area of Interstate 80 and Mockingbird Drive. They said that authorities from Omaha police and Game and Parks are monitoring the area. The woman whose camera caught the cat on Monday morning called Game and Parks, who confirmed the sighting and searched the area. Sam Wilson with Game and Parks told KETV NewsWatch 7 that they searched both north and south of the area the mountain lion was caught on camera. He said with no new signs of activity, the search was turned over to Omaha police. If you do see a mountain lion, call Omaha police or Game and Parks.
