 The U.S. Postal Inspection Service is offering up to $50,000 for information on several mail carrier vehicle robberies and a further $10,000 for information on the theft of keys from the mail carriers. The postal service reported six thefts in municipalities across the state from November of 2021 to December of 2022. There were reported robberies of the vehicles in West Haven on Nov. 11, 2021, in Hartford on March 3, in Waterbury on Sept. 30, in Middletown on Nov. 1, in Greenwich on Nov. 10 and in Stamford on Dec. 7. Hartford police responded to a reported armed robbery on March 3 around 4 p.m. The victim, who was a UPS mail carrier, reported that he was robbed at gunpoint by two juveniles while he was delivering mail on Newbury Street in Hartford, police said. The two juveniles approached him, took out a gun and demanded the keys to the delivery vehicle, police said. The victim complied with the two juveniles, but the suspect with the firearm hit him in the head. The two minors left the area in a silver sedan and did not steal the truck, police said. The mail carrier sustained minor head injuries in the altercation, police said. Anyone with information on the robberies is asked to contact the U.S. Postal Inspection Service at 1-877-876-2455 and say “law enforcement.” Copyright © 2024 Hartford Courant
