Metadata is the information that describes, categorizes, and organizes your documents. It can include details such as title, author, date, keywords, format, source, and more. Metadata helps you find, manage, and share your documents more efficiently and effectively. But how do you ensure that your metadata is consistent and accurate across different document types and sources? Here are some tips to help you improve your document management metadata.
 
The first step is to define your metadata standards and requirements for your document types and sources. This means identifying what metadata elements are mandatory, optional, or conditional, and how they should be formatted, validated, and controlled. For example, you may have different metadata standards for PDFs, Word documents, images, and web pages. You may also have different metadata standards for internal and external documents, or for different departments or projects. Having clear and consistent metadata standards will help you avoid errors, confusion, and duplication.
