 The healthcare industry is a complex and multifaceted field that requires efficient management of various processes to ensure smooth operations and accurate financial transactions. Two critical aspects of healthcare administration are medical coding and billing. While both play crucial roles in the revenue cycle of healthcare organizations, they serve distinct purposes and involve different responsibilities. In this blog, we will explore the difference between medical coding and billing to shed light on these essential components of the healthcare ecosystem. Medical coding is the process of assigning standardized codes to medical diagnoses, procedures, and services. These codes are derived from universally recognised code sets, such as the International Classification of Diseases (ICD) and the Current Procedural Terminology (CPT). Medical coders are responsible for reviewing medical documentation, extracting relevant information, and assigning appropriate codes based on the provided guidelines. The primary purpose of medical coding is to accurately represent the patient’s medical condition and the services provided during their encounter with a healthcare provider. These codes serve multiple functions, including facilitating proper reimbursement, supporting medical research and analysis, and ensuring compliance with regulatory requirements.
