AppsFlyer and Google have recently released a report which examines how mobile apps are impacting finance in Africa.  The report found that there has been a significant rise in the number of finance app installs across the continent, as more people turn to technology to manage their finances and navigate economic uncertainty.  The report analyzed over 140 million installs across 3,000 finance apps and found that installs grew by 25%. Ghana, Nigeria, Kenya and South Africa were the main contributors to this increase, with Ghana seeing a 200% growth in installs.  Google search trends between May 2021 and May 2022 showed that lending and currency conversions were the most searched finance-related topics, indicating the economic uncertainty in Africa, particularly post-pandemic.
