 Welcome to the 24th edition of the Zofi Cash newsletter. The week was interrupted by Eid, which came in clutch for a well deserved rest in the middle of the week. But the work never stops. In today's newsletter, we talk about fintechs can utilise social media to acquire customers and also AI to maximise their user experience. We also talk about internet poverty along gender lines and celebrate our late July babies. Read our newsletter below.  In Uganda, social media has witnessed a remarkable surge in popularity, transforming the way people connect, consume information, and make purchasing decisions. As the fintech industry in Uganda continues to grow, fintechs will continue to seek ways in which they can acquire customers at a low cost. One of these ways will be through harnessing the power of social media This article delves into the significance of social media for fintech customer acquisition in Uganda. Read the full story here. Financial technology, commonly known as fintech, refers to the innovative use of technology in providing financial services. Fintech companies, like Zofi Cash, are reshaping the financial landscape by leveraging cutting-edge technologies to enhance customer experiences, automate processes, and improve operational efficiency. One such trans-formative technology is artificial intelligence (AI), which has revolutionised various sectors across the globe. This essay examines how AI has transformed other industries and delving into the ways in which Ugandan fintechs can leverage AI to enhance customer experiences, automate processes, and detect fraud. Read the full story here.  Chart of the Week Uganda has at least 31 million internet poor people according to World Data Labs. Internet access in Uganda is crucial as it enables individuals to access information, enhance education, and stay informed. It promotes economic growth, facilitates communication and social connections, fosters civic participation, and empowers individuals to drive development and advance democracy. Toady, we did a gender break-down of internet poor people and it is almost 50/50. There are 500,000 more internet poor women than men.To end internet poverty in Uganda, efforts should be made to improve infrastructure, expand affordable internet access, and provide digital literacy programs to ensure widespread and inclusive connectivity for all.
