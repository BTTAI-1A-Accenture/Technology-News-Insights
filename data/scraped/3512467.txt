Ayesha Shroff, mother of Bollywood actor Tiger Shroff, has filed a police complaint alleging that she was duped of  ₹ 58.53 lakh by a kickboxer who was working as a director of operations in her son's mixed martial arts (MMA) training company. The accused, identified as Alan Fernandes, was appointed as director of operations in Tiger Shroff's MMA Matrix company in 2018. He is accused of collecting money to organize 11 tournaments in India and abroad through the firm and depositing  ₹ 58.53 lakh in his personal account. Ayesha Shroff lodged a complaint with the Santacruz police on May 3, after which Fernandes was booked under Indian Penal Code sections for cheating, criminal breach of trust and other offences. The police are currently investigating the matter and are trying to track down Fernandes. "Fernandes was appointed director in the MMA Matrix firm in 2018. He is accused of collecting money to organise 11 tournaments in India and abroad through the firm and depositing  ₹58.53 lakh in his personal account," the official said quoting the FIR.
