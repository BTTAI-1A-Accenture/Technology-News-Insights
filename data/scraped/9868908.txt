ARSENAL will face Portuguese side Sporting Lisbon in the last 16 of the Europa League. Manchester United, meanwhile, have drawn Real Betis. The Red Devils booked their place in the last 16 with a dramatic 4-3 aggregate win over fellow European giants Barcelona. While Arsenal enjoyed a more serene route, sparing themselves the play-off round by winning their group prior to the World Cup. The Gunners' opponents Sporting Lisbon dropped out of the Champions League at the expense of Eintracht Frankfurt and Arsenal's bitter rivals Tottenham. They boast former Spur Marcus Edwards, who shone against his old side despite Sporting being knocked out. After coming third in their Champions League group, Sporting were forced to take on Danish side FC Midtjylland in the Europa play-off. They drew 1-1 in the first leg in Portugal, before an emphatic 4-0 victory in Denmark. United's opponents Real Betis, meanwhile, emphatically won Europa League Group C under the guidance of ex-Man City boss Manuel Pellegrini. They overcame Jose Mourinho's Roma in the process, as well as Ludogorets and HJK. Elsewhere in the draw, Italian giants Juventus landed German side Freiburg.  While Europa League specialists Sevilla will face Turkish outfit Fenerbahce. Roma were drawn with LaLiga side Real Sociedad, with Bundesliga surprise package Union Berlin taking on Belgian last 16 debutants Union Saint-Gilloise. Bayer Leverkusen take on Hungarians Ferencvaros, who will host their home leg at Puskas Arena, the venue that this year's final will be played at. While Shakhtar Donetsk face Dutch side Feyenoord. Meanwhile, in the Europa Conference League, West Ham will face Cypriot side AEK Larnaca. David Moyes' side reached the Europa League semi-finals last term, but are struggling in the Prem.
