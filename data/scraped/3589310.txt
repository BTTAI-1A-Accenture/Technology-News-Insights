Paints and coatings are both types of surface finishes applied to various materials, primarily to protect and enhance the appearance of the underlying surface. While they share similarities, they have distinct characteristics and purposes: Paints: Coatings: Coatings can be applied to a wide range of materials and products, including metals, plastics, ceramics, glass, and fabrics. They are used in various industries, such as automotive, aerospace, marine, construction, electronics, and textiles, to enhance the durability and performance of products and surfaces. In summary, paints are a specific type of coating that contains pigments and binders and is primarily used for decorative and protective purposes on surfaces like walls and furniture. Coatings, on the other hand, encompass a broader category of materials applied to different surfaces for protective and functional purposes. The company operates through three business segments, namely, performance coatings, industrial coatings, and glass. It caters to the needs of a number of end-use industries, such as construction, consumer products, industrial, and transportation. The company offers automotive coatings under its industrial coatings segment. It has a presence in North America, Europe, Asia Pacific, Latin America, the Middle East, and Africa, with operations in over 156 countries. THE SHERWIN-WILLIAMS MANUFACTURING COMPANY: Sherwin-Williams is a diversified coatings company and a leading supplier of advanced technology liquid and powder coatings for the global OEM market through 15 manufacturing and 79 facility locations across the US, Canada, Mexico, and China. The company has a wide product portfolio, which includes brands, such as Sherwin-Williams, HGTV HOME by Dutch Boy, Krylon, Minwax, and Thompson's Water Seal.
