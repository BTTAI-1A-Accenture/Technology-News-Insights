 High-caliber candidates are primarily drawn to your company when it possesses an appealing allure. However, what precisely contributes to the attractiveness of a company? The top 10 take-aways from our candidate discussions: Inclusive and Diverse Culture: Companies that prioritize diversity and inclusion create a welcoming environment for employees of all backgrounds. This includes promoting gender equality, cultural diversity, and ensuring equal opportunities for all. Flexible Work Arrangements: The future workforce values flexibility in work arrangements. Offering options for remote work, flexible hours, and job-sharing can attract a wider range of talent, including those seeking a better work-life balance. Professional Development and Opportunities for Leadership: Continuous learning and growth opportunities are crucial. Companies that invest in employee training, mentorship programs, and opportunities for skill development will be more appealing to individuals looking to advance their careers. Workplace Wellbeing and Transparent Communication: Employers that prioritize the physical, mental, and emotional wellbeing of their employees will stand out. This includes initiatives such as wellness programs, mental health support, and a focus on work-life integration. Purpose-Driven Mission: Employees increasingly want to work for companies that have a positive impact on society. Having a clear and meaningful mission that aligns with societal values can attract individuals who seek purpose in their work. Innovative Technology: Companies that leverage cutting-edge technology to enhance work processes and employee experiences demonstrate a commitment to staying ahead of the curve. Competitive Compensation: While not the sole factor, competitive salaries and benefits remain important for attracting and retaining top talent. Strong Employer Branding: A well-defined and positive employer brand can set a company apart from competitors and attract candidates who resonate with its values and culture. Feedback and Recognition: Regular feedback, performance evaluations, and recognition for a job well done contribute to employee satisfaction. Recognizing and valuing employee contributions can foster a positive work environment. Environmental Sustainability: Future employees are likely to gravitate toward environmentally responsible companies. Demonstrating a commitment to sustainability through eco-friendly practices can attract environmentally-conscious individuals.  Vice President at DHL Spot on, Martina. Couldn't agree more. However, it’s only possible if all (or at least most) employees live them. If there are any uncertainties or obscurities, especially in the lower management, these take-aways are just printed letters on a paper.

I also would add one 11th point: in addition to mentoring projects and a great feedback culture, also a positive failure culture should be implemented. It opens a great space for employees to learn and grow.
