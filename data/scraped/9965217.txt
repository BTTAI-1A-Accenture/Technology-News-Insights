HAWKINS COUNTY, Tenn. — Hawkins County officials said Verizon callers are able to access 911 again after reporting an issue that's impacting customers Monday. Hawkins County 911 said Verizon customers were having issues connecting to 911. People with emergencies who are unable to connect to 911 should call the non-emergency line at (423) 272-7121. The county said the issue was specifically with Verizon, saying the company worked to fix the issue. The 911 center said other emergency communications districts in the state reported the same issue.
