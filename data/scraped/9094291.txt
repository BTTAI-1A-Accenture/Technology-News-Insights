WEATHER ALERT Christie Zizo, Digital Editor Published: March 16, 2023 at 2:23 PM Updated: March 17, 2023 at 7:49 PM Christie Zizo, Digital Editor CAPE CANAVERAL, Fla. – SpaceX successfully pulled off a sunset launch from Cape Canaveral Space Force Station on Friday. The Falcon 9 rocket launched from Space Launch Complex 40 at 7:38 p.m. The rocket also launched the SES18 and SES19 satellites in a geosynchronous transfer orbit. Recommended Videos The satellites are part of an effort to improve communication access in the United States. The first stage of the Falcon 9 rocket later landed on a SpaceX droneship out in the Atlantic Ocean. [TRENDING: Become a News 6 Insider] Weather for the launch was 80% go with the main concern being for liftoff winds. If there were a problem with the launch, SpaceX planned to make another attempt at the same time on Saturday.
