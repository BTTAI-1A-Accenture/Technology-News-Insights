   Date (MENAFN- PRLog)   PITTSBURGH  -  March 20, 2023  - prlog -- JKS Financial, a financial services firm with Northwestern Mutual, continues its support of the Pittsburgh community by partnering with the University of Pittsburgh to sponsor the school's Rebound Rewards program throughout the 2022-2023 men's basketball season. As part of this partnership, JKS Financial, together with Northwestern Mutual, donated more than $10,000 to United Way of Southwestern Pennsylvania.Through the Rebound Rewards program, JKS Financial committed to a $10 donation for every rebound recorded by the Pittsburgh Panthers during the regular men's basketball season. This year, the Panthers recorded a total of 1,134 rebounds, securing a donation of $11,340 for United Way of Southwestern Pennsylvania.JKS Financial sponsors Rebound Rewards as part of its mission to help support the local community in obtaining financial freedom and success. The mission of United Way of Southwestern Pennsylvania, a nonprofit organization that mobilizes donor communities in creating equitable opportunities for all, aligns with that of JKS Financial. Both organizations are committed to increasing employment opportunities and aiding individuals of all ages and abilities in finding financial independence.The donation was presented with Northwestern Mutual to United Way of Southwestern Pennsylvania during the University of Pittsburgh's final home game against Syracuse on February 25, 2023. To learn more and support the United Way of Southwestern Pennsylvania and its mission to improve lives within local communities, please visit uwswpa.org.  About JKS Financial  JKS Financial is a Pittsburgh, PA-based financial planning practice for wealth management advisors, Geoff Kasse and Dan Jenkins. Kasse and Jenkins have more than 55 years of combined experience and offer wealth accumulation, insurance and risk management, wealth preservation, and wealth distribution services. JKS Financial's mission is to provide sound financial planning for all aspects of your wealth management needs.
