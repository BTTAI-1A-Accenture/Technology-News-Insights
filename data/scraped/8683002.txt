Giant telecommunications company Safaricom has projected that it will lose at least KSh 2 billion after the Communications Authority of Kenya (CA) cut the mobile termination rate by 41.4%. CA defines mobile termination rate as a charge applied by a public telecommunications service provider for terminating a call. The regulator reduced the rate from KSh 0.99 to KSh 0.58 per minute in August 2022. Business Daily reported that in the half-year ended September, Safaricom recorded a KSh 470 million drop in interconnect revenue.
